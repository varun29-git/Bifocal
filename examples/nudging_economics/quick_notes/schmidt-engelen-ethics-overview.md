## Source Triage

- Type: Review and philosophical overview.
- Author Context: Clear. Andreas T. Schmidt, University of Groningen, and Bart Engelen, Tilburg University.
- Evidence Mode: Conceptual synthesis of the ethics debate, not an empirical effectiveness study.
- Use Level: Strong for definitions, ethical categories, and objections. Supporting only for empirical effectiveness claims.

## Signal

The paper defines nudging as changing choice architecture to predictably alter behavior without forbidding options or significantly changing economic incentives. It distinguishes nudges from incentives, coercion, and mere information provision.

The core ethical debate is not simply whether nudges work. It is whether they respect freedom of choice, volitional autonomy, rational agency, nondomination, dignity, and the need for structural reform.

## Open Question

Can nudges be ethically assessed as a class, or only case by case?

Concept Candidates:

- Nudge

- Structural reform

## Caveat

This source is balanced and useful for ethical framing, but it does not settle the empirical effectiveness question.
