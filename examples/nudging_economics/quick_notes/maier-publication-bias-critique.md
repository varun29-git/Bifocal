## Source Triage

- Type: Methodological critique and reanalysis letter.
- Author Context: Clear. Maximilian Maier, Frantisek Bartos, T. D. Stanley, David R. Shanks, Adam J. L. Harris, and Eric-Jan Wagenmakers.
- Evidence Mode: Reanalysis of Mertens et al. using publication-bias adjustment.
- Use Level: Strong counterevidence for broad claims about nudge effectiveness.

## Signal

The letter argues that publication bias is the central finding in the Mertens et al. meta-analysis. Using robust Bayesian meta-analysis, it claims that after adjustment, there is no remaining evidence that nudges are effective overall.

The critique does not imply that every individual nudge fails. It argues against the general claim that nudging is established as an effective behavior-change tool across the literature.

## Open Question

Which specific nudge categories or domains remain promising after publication-bias adjustment?

## Caveat

This is a short critique focused on one meta-analysis. It is powerful counterevidence, but a complete synthesis should also inspect replies, corrections, and later meta-analytic work if available.
