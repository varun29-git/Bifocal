# Observation: Meta-Analysis Finds Small To Medium Effects With Bias Concerns

Source: `sources/mertens-et-al-2021-the-effectiveness-of-nudging-a-meta-analysis-of-choice-architecture-interventions-across-behavioral.pdf`
Reviewed: 2026-05-08
Source Date: 2021-12-30

## Source Triage

- Type: Meta-analysis.
- Author Context: Clear. Stephanie Mertens, Mario Herberz, Ulf J. J. Hahnel, and Tobias Brosch, University of Geneva.
- Evidence Mode: Quantitative synthesis of more than 200 studies and more than 440 effect sizes.
- Use Level: Strong for the pro-effectiveness side, but must be paired with publication-bias critique.

## Signal

The paper finds that choice architecture interventions produce an overall small to medium effect on behavior, with variation by intervention category and behavioral domain. Decision-structure interventions outperform decision-information and decision-assistance interventions. Food choices appear especially responsive.

The paper also reports publication bias and substantial heterogeneity. Its own results imply that some nudges can backfire and that effectiveness depends on technique, domain, and context.

## Open Question

How much of the reported effectiveness survives strong publication-bias correction?

Concept Candidates:

- Choice architecture
- Decision structure
- Decision information
- Decision assistance
- Publication bias
- Backfire risk

## Caveat

This source is empirical and broad, but its headline effect should not be treated as settled because the literature shows publication bias and high heterogeneity.
