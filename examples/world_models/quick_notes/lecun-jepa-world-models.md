# Observation: JEPA Frames World Models As Predictive Abstraction

Source: `sources/A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence (JEPA).pdf`
Reviewed: 2026-05-08
Source Date: 2022-06-27

## Source Triage

- Type: Position paper and architecture proposal.
- Author Context: Clear. Yann LeCun, New York University and Meta Fundamental AI Research.
- Evidence Mode: Mostly conceptual argument and architecture proposal, with references to broader research. It asserts a research path more than it reports a benchmark result.
- Use Level: Context and conceptual framing. Do not use alone as empirical evidence.

## Signal

The JEPA paper broadens the meaning of world models beyond reinforcement learning benchmark agents. It argues that intelligent systems need predictive models of the world, hierarchical abstraction, self-supervised representation learning, intrinsic motivation, and planning over multiple time scales.

Its key contribution to this source set is a non-generative, joint-embedding route to predictive world models, contrasting with the generative latent dynamics style used in the Dreamer line.
