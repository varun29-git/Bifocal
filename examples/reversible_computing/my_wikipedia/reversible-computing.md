# Reversible Computing

Status: Under Review

## Working Definition

Reversible computing studies computation in which the physical or logical process avoids unnecessary information loss, so that earlier states can in principle be reconstructed from later states.

## Why It Matters

Information loss is connected to thermodynamic cost. Reversible computing matters because it asks whether computation can be organized to reduce entropy production and energy dissipation, especially as ordinary digital systems approach energy and heat limits.
