# Late Bronze Age Collapse

Status: Under Review

## Working Definition

The Late Bronze Age collapse was a broad period of disruption, decline, destruction, abandonment, and transformation across parts of the eastern Mediterranean and Near East between roughly the late 13th and 12th centuries BCE, followed by major Early Iron Age reorganization.

## Why It Matters

This is the central object of the example. The term "collapse" can hide regional variation, because some palatial centers fell sharply while other regions continued, adapted, or later flourished under new social and political forms.
