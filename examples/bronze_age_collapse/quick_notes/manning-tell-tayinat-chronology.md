# Observation: Tell Tayinat Shows Alternative Trajectories After Collapse

Source: `sources/Beyond megadrought and collapse in the Northern Levant.pdf`
Reviewed: 2026-05-08
Source Date: 2020-10-29

## Source Triage

- Type: Primary archaeological chronology study.
- Author Context: Clear. Sturt W. Manning, Brita Lorentzen, Lynn Welton, Stephen Batiuk, and Timothy P. Harrison, published in PLOS ONE.
- Evidence Mode: Radiocarbon dates integrated with stratigraphy using Bayesian chronological modeling.
- Use Level: Strong for regional timing and for resisting simple collapse narratives.

## Signal

The paper places Tell Tayinat inside two debated climate-crisis windows, around 2200 BCE and around 1200 BCE, but emphasizes varied local responses. For the Late Bronze to Iron Age transition, Tell Tayinat appears as an alternative developmental trajectory rather than a straightforward casualty of collapse.

This matters because it separates regional crisis from uniform destruction. The same broad stress period could produce abandonment in some places, reorientation in others, and new political formations elsewhere.

## Open Question

How many "collapse" claims disappear once local chronological and ecological variation is added?

## Caveat

This is a site-centered study in the northern Levant. It should not be generalized to the whole eastern Mediterranean without comparison to other regional evidence.
