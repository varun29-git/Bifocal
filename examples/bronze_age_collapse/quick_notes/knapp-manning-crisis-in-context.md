# Observation: Late Bronze Age Crisis Needs Multiple Causes

Source: `sources/aja.120.1.0099.pdf`
Reviewed: 2026-05-08
Source Date: 2016

## Source Triage

- Type: Scholarly review and synthesis.
- Author Context: Clear. A. Bernard Knapp and Sturt W. Manning, published in American Journal of Archaeology.
- Evidence Mode: Reviews archaeological, documentary, climatological, and chronological evidence.
- Use Level: Strong framing source for complexity, sequence, and evidence caution.

## Signal

The article argues that there is no single overarching explanation for the end of the Late Bronze Age in the eastern Mediterranean. It treats migration, warfare, trade disruption, political instability, climate stress, earthquakes, and disease as possible interacting factors rather than rival one-factor explanations.

The strongest caution is methodological: documentary, material, climatic, and chronological evidence do not line up cleanly enough to support a simple collapse story.
